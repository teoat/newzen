## 3. Forensic Interpretation

### 🚧 Technical Justification (Justek) Prediction

The massive shift from **Masonry (-637M)** to **Gabions (+2.1B)** strongly suggests **Hydrological Risk**.

- Rigid masonry walls fail in dynamic river beds due to scouring (undermining).
- Flexible gabions (bronjong) can settle without breaking, making them superior for unstable river banks.

### 🏗️ Structural Re-evaluation

The reduction in **Concrete (-978M)** and **Steel Frame (-752M)** paired with increased **Piling (+896M)** suggests a **Foundation-First Strategy**:

- The team likely discovered poorer-than-expected soil conditions (requiring deep piles).
- To fund this expensive foundation work without blowing the budget, they optimized the superstructure (reducing concrete/steel volume).
